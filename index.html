Among Us Project

Introduction


Among Us is a popular multiplayer video game for up to 10 people. It is available as a free mobile app on android and IOS or it is available on desktop ($4.99). Each game contains 4 to 10 players on a virtual spacecraft where there are 1-3 “impostors” and everyone else is a “crewmate.” Each player has a username and is represented by a different colored avatar. During gameplay, while crewmates are trying to carry out tasks throughout the ship, impostors may stealthily kill crewmates and use vents to quickly transport themselves throughout the ship. Crewmates may be able to identify an impostor if they witness them killing another player or traveling through a vent (venting). Players who are killed by impostors or who are ejected from the ship are then excluded from the main gameplay, but continue the game as ghosts where they can watch the game unfold, perform tasks to help the crew win, and chat with other dead players.

Players can have meetings when someone presses either the “emergency meeting” or “dead body report” buttons. In these meetings, players can chat with each other and then vote to eject (vote out) another player. If someone receives a majority of votes, they are ejected into space by the rest of the crew. The goal for crewmates is to identify and eject the impostor, but the impostor(s) may try to sabotage the process and persuade the crew to accuse and eject an innocent crewmate.

The nature of the game typically inspires paranoia and confusion throughout the crew. Actions that could be carried out by either an impostor or a crewmate like traveling alone, reporting a dead body, being too quiet, or making accusations may be deemed “sus” by the rest of the crew.

While skills in navigating the ship, completing tasks, and maintaining an awareness of surroundings are valuable in Among Us, the game is largely based on social and psychological elements. Crewmates must balance relying on mutual trust with a healthy suspicion of other players in order to work together to sniff out an impostor. Even experienced players can be fooled by impostors, or wrongly accused of being an impostor themselves. People who play regularly with groups of close friends may begin to identify patterns and tells, developing their skills based on what they know about fellow players and their techniques.

Maps

In Among Us, there are three different maps (The Skeld, Mira HQ and Polus Maps):

The Skeld Map

The Structure

1. Cafeteria: It is the spawn point on this map. There are five lunch tables. It is connected to three hallways and has a vent. Players can press the Emergency Meeting button on the center table to call an Emergency Meeting. It has three tasks:
- Empty Garbage (Top Right): This is a two part task, with the first switch right next to the Upload Data task. Click the switch, then hold down the lever to empty the chute. After this, head down to the Storage room to repeat the task.
- Download Data (Top Right): One of those data tasks that finishes in the Admin room begins in the Cafeteria. Select the WiFi board in the top right corner, click upload, and wait a few seconds for the transfer. Afterwards, head to the Admin room to upload it. This process takes around 8.7 seconds.
- Fix Wiring (Top Left): click the panel then line up the wires across either side by matching the colors.

2. Weapons: It is located to the cafeteria's right, the weapons room is connected to two hallways and has a vent. It has three tasks:
- Clear Asteroids (Center, Visual Task): Several asteroids will come flying past the screen once you click the seat and joy sticks in the middle; hit 20 of them to complete this task.
- Download Data (Top Left): Just like download data in Cafeteria, select the WiFi board in the top right corner, click upload, and wait a few seconds for the transfer. Afterwards, head to the Admin room to upload it. This process takes around 8.7 seconds
- Accept Diverted Power (Right): the player must locate the panel to accept the diverted power and click or flip the metal part in the middle to complete the task.

3. Navigation: The navigation room contains the steering and course chart stations, an electrical acceptance panel, and a data point. There is also an electrical wiring panel in the hallway outside Navigation. There are two vents at the top and bottom of the room. It has five tasks:
- Chart Course (Top Console): There are two control panels at the back of Navigation. The left hand one lets you chart a course, and you need to zig zag the ship along the lines provided.
- Stabilize Steering (Middle Console):
- Upload Data (Top Right): select the WiFi board in the top right corner, click upload, and wait a few seconds for the transfer. Afterwards, head to the Admin room to upload it. This process takes around 8.7 seconds
- Fix Wiring (Left): click the panel then line up the wires across either side by matching the colors.
- Divert Power (Top Left): The player must move the highlighted switch to the top until it can be moves no more. Moving it towards the bottom decreases the power.

4. O2: The O2 room houses a trash emptier, an O2vent, and a number pad for fixing the Oxygen Deprivation sabotage. This room is connected to a single hallway.It has three tasks
- Clean O2 Filter (Top Left): Click and drag the leaves out of the grate.
- Empty Chute (Bottom Left): Pull the lever on the back wall to empty the chute. The second part of the task takes place in Storage.
- Stop Oxygen Depletion (Top right, only occurs when the Impostor sabotages it): players need to enter the correct codes on two PIN pads in Admin and O2.

5. Shields: The shields room is connected to two hallways and has a vent.
Tasks:
- Prime Shields (Bottom Left, Visual): This is the little speaker box in the bottom corner of the room. Select it and click each of the red hexagons.
- Accept Diverted Power: The player must locate the panel to accept the diverted power and click or flip the metal part in the middle to complete the task.

6. Communications (comms): The communications room has a data point, an electrical acceptance panel, and a computer to fix the Comms sabotage. It is connected to a single hallway. It has three tasks:
- Download Data: select the WiFi board in the top right corner, click upload, and wait a few seconds for the transfer. Afterwards, head to the Admin room to upload it. This process takes around 8.7 seconds
- Accept Diverted Power: The player must locate the panel to accept the diverted power and click or flip the metal part in the middle to complete the task.
- Fix Communications (Only occurs when the Impostor sabotages it): the communications are restored by adjusting the dial to match two wavelengths on a monitor.

7. Storage: The storage room has containers in the middle of the room, which obstruct vision to the other side. It is connected to three hallways.It has three tasks:
- Fuel Engines - Gas canister: Fill their gas can by holding the silver button to the right of the can.
- Fix Wiring (Top Left): Click the panel then line up the wires across either side by matching the colors
- Empty Garbage/Chute (Bottom Right, Visual): click the switch, then hold down the lever to empty the chute/garbage.

8. Admin: The admin room contains a large table in the center of the room, a card-swiper, and a map of players in each room. The Admin room also houses a data upload point, an electrical wiring panel, and a number pad for fixing the Oxygen Deprivation sabotage. It is connected to one hallway and has a vent. It has three tasks:
- Swipe Card: the player must swipe the card inside the wallet on the draw frame. The card needs to be timed correctly. Swiping too slow or too fast or taking their hand off earlier will fail to complete the task, and consequently, the player must try again.
- Upload Data: click the upload button and wait for the upload. It takes around 8.7 seconds to finish this task.
- Stop Oxygen Depletion (Only occurs when the Impostor sabotages it): players need to enter the correct codes on two PIN pads in Admin and O2

9. Electrical: The electrical room contains the distributor, an electrical divergence panel, an electrical wiring panel, and a data point. There is also an electrical panel to fix the light sabotage. It is connected to one hallway and has a vent. It has five tasks:
- Divert Power to (Top Left): Select the control panel, and then flip the switch to divert power to whichever room you need. From there, you just need to find the panel (the yellow arrow will guide you) and flip the connector in the center.
- Fix Wiring (Top Middle): Click the panel then line up the wires across either side by matching the colors.
- Calibrate Distributor (Top Right): Select the box with the high voltage sign on the right hand side of the room and line up the three rectangles spinning around the circle. They all have to stop on the right, connected to the distributor plugs.
- Download Data: Select the WiFi board in the top right corner, click upload, and wait a few seconds for the transfer. Afterwards, head to the Admin room to upload it. This process takes around 8.7 seconds
- Fix Lights (Only occurs when the Impostor sabotages it): The players need to flip the switches on a circuit breaker in Electrical and make the small light above each switch turn bright green.

10. Lower engine: The lower engine has engine alignment and engine fueling stations, and an electrical acceptance panel. It is connected to two hallways and has a vent. It has two tasks:
- Align Engine Output: The crewmates must move the engine close enough to align with the middle line of the object.
- Fuel Engines: Fill their gas can by holding the silver button to the right of the can.
- Accept Diverted Power: The player must locate the panel to accept the diverted power and click or flip the metal part in the middle to complete the task.

11.Security: The security room has a camera station and one electrical acceptance panel. It is connected to one hallway and has a vent. It has two tasks:
- Accept Diverted Power: The player must locate the panel to accept the diverted power and click or flip the metal part in the middle to complete the task.
- Fix Wiring: Click the panel then line up the wires across either side by matching the colors.

12. Reactor: The reactor contains the reactor starting controls, the reactor manifold controls, and an electrical acceptance panel. The reactor also has two hand readers to fix the Reactor Meltdown sabotage. It is connected to one hallway and has two vents.It has thee tasks:
- Start Reactor: This is the control panel next to the glowing tubes (ie the reactor). There is a sequence, first with one flashing light adding one each time until five flashing lights, that needs to be repeated in order to to complete the task.
- Unlock Manifolds (Top): This task is the little bar at the back of the room. Select it and then click the numbers 1-10 in order.
- Stop Reactor Meltdown (Requires two alive players): To fix this sabotage, two players will need to hold their hands on two separate scanners at the Reactor at the same time.

13. Upper engine: The upper engine has engine alignment and fuelling stations and an electrical acceptance panel. It is connected to two hallways and has a vent.It has three tasks:
- Align Engine Output: The crewmates must move the engine close enough to align with the middle line of the object.
- Fuel Engines: Fill their gas can by holding the silver button to the right of the can.
- Accept Diverted Power: The player must locate the panel to accept the diverted power and click or flip the metal part in the middle to complete the task.

14. Medbay: The MedBay is connected to one hallway and has a vent. It has two tasks:
- Inspect Sample: It has two stage. In stage 1, a machine holding 5 test tubes will appear. The player must press the small green button on the bottom right of the machine. Then players must wait for 60 seconds. After that, the player will need to return to the machine to complete the task. The player must press the green button below the red sample. If done successfully, the top of the machine will read, "THANK YOU!" and the bottom will read "TEST COMPLETE." If player fails (one of the four blue samples are selected), the machine will read "BAD RESULT," the green buttons will flash red, and the entire task will need to be done again.
- Submit Scan (Visual): This task consists of the player standing on top of a capsule that starts to scan themself, while their information is shown, all in about 10 seconds. While doing the task, a hologram that scans the player is shown both to themself and other players. Multiple players may not do the scan simultaneously and, instead, have to wait in a queue for the player who initiated the task first to finish.






The Vent System:
- Admin, cafeteria, and the hallway above shields are connected.
- Electrical, MedBay, and security are connected.
- Weapons and shields are connected to navigation.
- The upper engine and lower engine are connected to the reactor.

The Visual Tasks: Visual Tasks are Tasks that other players can see being performed. Seeing someone do a Visual Task is proof that they are not an Impostor since Impostors can't do any tasks. Visual effects for tasks can be turned off by the host in the game options. There are Four visual Tasks in Skeld Map:
- Empty Garbage (Storage)
- Clear Asteroids (In Weapons)
- Submit Scan (Med Bay)
- Prime Shields (Shields)




Sabotage locations:There a lot of ways to sabotage in Skeld map ( see picture below )



One of them is to lockdown a room (to shut a room's door), that will delay the crew’s progress or prevent them from escape. Upper Engine, Lower Engine, Security, MedBay, Electrical, Cafeteria, and Storage can all be locked down.

You can also set off the Reactor, which will kill the crew if not disabled in time, turn off the O2 which will suffocate them if not fixed, turn off the lights to impair vision, or take out the communications to disable the security cameras.






The Polus Map

Polus’ is a large, snowy planet with 15 rooms and is the largest map in Among Us. Walking around this map is a little different the others, as it features a large, open space in the middle. Some of the key differences are:
- Polus is the only map without a cafeteria, as The Skeld and MIRA HQ both have cafeterias, and it is replaced by the Office.
- The doors do not open automatically, they can be opened when interacting. There are four disabled buttons, which, when pressing them, will open the door. - Security is similar to The Skeld; however, the difference is that it consists of only one screen, and players must change the camera view by pressing the buttons, and the cameras have an even greater field of view than that of The Skeld, which allows players to see inside the corridors, something that didn't appear on previous maps.
- The Reactors on this map are called Seismic Stabilizers; however, they still use the Reactor icon.
- Vents appear as holes in the ground.

The structure: : This map has 15 rooms:

1. Boiler Room: It is a room encompassed in O2. It has many wires at the walls and a water jug. It has two task:
- Replace Water Jug: Fill up a water jug by holding down the button until all of the water is transferred from the jug's right side to the left.
- Open Water Ways: The first two parts of this task are both in the Boiler Room, and involve the valves at the bottom of the room. Click each in turn, and spin them until the water bar at the side rises.

2. Communications: This room is located near Weapons. It is a small room with a small table and a big screen to Upload Data, and a WiFi Panel at the left to Reboot Wifi when having the Task. It has two tasks:
- Upload Data: Click upload data and wait until this process complete. It takes around 8.7 seconds complete.
- Reboot Wifi: Find the communication box. The screen will read "Reboot Required." The player must pull the lever, which will begin a 60-second countdown process. The Wi-Fi will power off, and the "Tasks" tab will display the remaining amount of seconds left in the countdown, along with the screen. After the countdown reaches 0, the crewmate must return to Communications to pull the lever back up to power on the Wi-Fi. This will complete the task.

3. Dropship: The Dropship is the main spawn point for players when they start the match, which is practically the lobby of a match before starting it. It has two tasks:
- Chart Course: Click the pad to the left of the back door. Drag the ship along the dotted course.
- Insert Key - Click the panel to the right of the back door to bring up the key slots. Select the glowing one, then click again to turn the key.

4. Electrical: electrical is an upside-down L-shaped room at the top-left corner of the map. It has an exterior section that contains the actual electrical equipment. Under that is vent. It has a door at the top-right of it that leads outside, a door at the bottom of it that leads to O2, and a doorway at the top-left that leads to Security. It has two tasks:
- Upload Data: Click the panel to the right of the back window and select ‘upload’. After this, follow the yellow arrow to Communications to transfer the data.
- Fix Wiring: Click the panel to the left of the back window, then match up the wires using the right colors. After this, you’ll need to head to the back wall as you enter O2 and the toilets in Laboratory to finish the task.

5. Laboratory: The Laboratory on Polus is a massive room encompassing several smaller rooms. In its center is the main laboratory room. Right below are bathrooms, which have a vent inside one of the stalls, leading directly outside and indirectly into the office. On the left are both the weather station and the laser drill pod. Each features several tasks connected to a hallway right below them, which is the left entrance to the labs. The entrance and the drill pod have a camera right outside. On the right, there is a small Medbay featuring a scanner. Right below is the upper decontamination room (its entrance facing the bathrooms), which leads to the Specimen Room. It has three tasks:
- Record Temperature: The thermostat will be in front of you as you enter. Click it and change the temperature on the left until it matches the one on the right.
- Repair Drill: Select the drill in the outer corner of the Laboratory. You’ll need to keep clicking on the four red corners until they disappear.
- Align Telescope: Click the telescope, then move it around until you find the object highlighted in the bottom corner.

6. Medbay: Otherwise, it's encompassed by the Laboratory. It contains a scanner and hospital beds. The beds look like they have a table next to them. It also has two tasks:
- Inspect Sample: Select the sample tray at the back on MedBay, then get ready to wait 60 seconds for it to process. During this time, you should go and do another task. Once time is up, head back and select the odd sample out.
- Submit Scan: Stand on the platform in the MedBay and click for the the scan to begin.

7. O2: On Polus, O2 takes up the space of three rooms, two medium-sized and one small. It is located above the Boiler Room. The top-left room contains the first stage of the Upload Data task, the Monitor Tree task, and a vent. The main room contains the Empty Garbage and Fix Wiring tasks, and the small room contains the Fill Canisters task. It has four tasks:
- Empty Garbage: Pull the lever in front of you as you enter O2, and hold it down until the garbage is empty.
- Upload Data: Click the panel behind the tree. Head to Communications afterwards to finish the task.
- Fill Canisters: In the bottom left corner of O2, click the terminal. Click and hold until the canister is full; do this this twice.
- Monitor Tree: Click the computer attached to the tree. Line up the four columns to make sure the slider sits at the top of the colored bars.

8. Office: The office takes up the space of two long rooms: one labeled as "Office," which features the Emergency Button and the second part of Replace Water Jug, and another housing vitals and all the other tasks. It has three tasks:
- Swipe Card: In the right room of the office, select the left terminal. Pick your card from your wallet and swipe it through.
- Scan Boarding Pass - Choose the middle terminal. Click the yellow arrow to the left to bring up your key card, then click the boarding pass to flip it over. Hold it over the scanner to finish the task.
- Upload Data - To the right of all the terminals, click the panel. Once uploaded, take the data to Communications.

9. Exterior (Outside): The exterior is the outer area of the planetary base, being located practically from the right exit of the Office, with a lava pit, several pilasters of climatic modules, among other things. There is a metal plank leading out to the lava, which is used to vote off and kill suspected impostors during the ejection scene. However, whilst playing, players cannot fall into the lava. This area can also be seen in the security cameras. It has two tasks:
- Weather Nodes - There are several weather nodes outside, some near the top, some near the bottom, and some more central. Use the map to see where the broken one is, then click it and drag the current through the maze. After this, head to the left side of the Laboratory to complete the calibration.
- Record Temperature - Head down to the bottom right by the pool of lava. Click the panel next to it and change the temperature on the left until it matches the temperature on the right.

10. Specimen room: The Specimen Room is located near the bottom right of the map. To access it, you have to pass through one of the small Decontamination rooms in either Admin or the Laboratory, then walk through a long empty hallway. The room features windows that let you see the exterior from inside. It has four tasks:
- Start Reactor: This is the bulky terminal on the back wall. Click it then repeat the flashing pattern five times.
- Upload Data: This is on the right hand side as soon as you enter from the Laboratory. Once this part is finished, you need to head to Communications.
- Unlock Manifolds: This is the thin terminal on the back wall. Click it then choose 1-10 in ascending order.
- Sort Artefacts: This is the box on the side wall. Choose it and place the four objects into the correctly shaped holes.

11. Storage: In Polus map, Storage is a small, utterly isolated room with only a gallon of fuel and a few boxes, along with a hole, which is connected to the holes in the Office and the one near Communications. There is a window which be viewed both inside and outside storage located at the bottom of the structure. It has one task:
- Fuel Engines: Fill the gas can by holding the silver button to the right of the can.

12. Weapons: It has two tasks:
- Destroy Asteroids: Select the console on the left of the room, and click to shoot 20 asteroids.
- Upload Data: On the right as soon as you enter. Take the data to Communications to complete the task.

13. Security: The Security room is a tiny room on the upper left side of the map. It has a computer screen that allows you to access cameras. The camera consists of only one screen, and players must change the camera view by pressing the buttons, and the cameras have an even greater field of view than that of The Skeld, which allows players to see inside the corridors.

14. Admin: In Polus map, Admin room is much bigger and features no task. It features the same Admin table as The Skeld, which here can be used on either side. It has a vent on the left-bottom corner, in a corridor-like formation, which leads directly outside the left wall or far off near the Laboratory entrance. Right below the Admin table is one of the two entrances to the Specimen Room.

15. Decontamination:: In Polus, there are two decontamination rooms in the map, each on either side of two hallways that extend out from the Specimen Room, one that goes up to the Laboratory, and one that goes left and down to Admin. They are tiny, square-shaped rooms and the one near the Laboratory has an electrical panel for the Fix Wiring task.



The vent system:While still called vents, in Porus its actually holes in the ground as mentioned above, though access remains restricted to Impostors only. There are a few interconnected systems, with their pathways described below:
- Office - Storage - Outside (near Communications)
- O2 - Electrical - Outside (near Electrical)
- Outside (left of Dropship) - Outsider (right of Dropship)
- Laboratory - Outside (near Laboratory) - Admin - Outside (near Admin)

Visual Task:visual tasks are tasks that other players can see being performed. Seeing someone do a Visual Task is proof that they are not an Impostor since Impostors can't do any tasks. Visual effects for tasks can be turned off by the host in the game options. In Polus Map, only 1 visual task which is med bay scan.

The Miras HQ Map

Mira HQ is one of three maps in Among Us, and is probably the second most popular after The Skeld. It was officially announced on August 3, 2019, along with a Henry Stickmin Collection update. It was released on August 8, 2019, with a trailer uploaded on August 9, 2019.
The structure:: This map has 14 rooms:

1. Admin: In MIRA HQ, the admin room is located under the Greenhouse, it has three tasks:
- Prime Shields - From the console at the bottom of the room, click and choose all of the red shapes.
- Chart Course - Go to the controls at the right corner of the room, and drag the ship along the dotted course.
- ID Code - Select the table as soon as you enter to bring up the code entry. Select the card from your wallet and enter the code printed on it into the key pad.

2. Balcony: It is at the bottom of the MIRA HQ, with the Clear Asteroids task on the left, the Measure Weather task on the right, and a vent in the middle between the two doors:
- Clear Asteroids: At the console to the left of the balcony, click to activate the asteroids then keep shooting until you’ve cleared 20.
- Measure Weather: Go the right of the balcony. Click the switch, then activate the terminal and wait for it to finish measuring.

3. Cafeteria: In MIRA HQ, the Cafeteria is located in the right corridor and is smaller than Skield map. It is also near the Storage and the Balcony, and the tables are cubic instead of circular and are light brown. A vent is located near the entrance, and the emergency button is located on the left table. It has 2 tasks:
- Empty Garbage: Head to the lever on the wall and hold it down until the screen is empty.
- Buy Beverage: Click the vending machine at the back of the room. There will be a scribbled drawing of the beverage to buy; find it and input the right code.

4. Communications: it is the location where the base receives communications from other MIRA spacecraft. It has several radio equipment and a computer that serves as a sensor that guides people's movement in the area. You can use Use the door log in commnunication room to track crew movements around the ship. It doesn't have any tasks

5. Greenhouse: It has one task:
- Clean O2 Filter - Click the glass chamber at the back of the room and drag all of the leaves out of the vent.

6. Locker Room: It is the place where the crew changes clothes. It has several lockers and a bench, and a pipe under the door that takes players to decontamination. It has one task:
- Fix Wiring: click the panel then line up the wires across either side by matching the colors. After this, you’ll need to head to the Greenhouse and to the Reactor to finish the task.

7. Laboratory: In MIRA HQ map, the Laboratory is a medium room, located across the Reactor after the decontamination hallway. It features a vent directly connected to other vents in the decontamination hallway, the Reactor, and the Office, and is part of MIRA HQ's vent network. It has 2 tasks:
- Assemble Artifact: This happens on the table at the top of the room. You’ll need to arrange the pieces of crystal into a jigsaw to finish the tasks.
- Sort Samples: at the bottom table, there are three boxes. You’ll need to put shiny geodes in the gem box, animal fossils in the paw print box, and plant fossils in the leaf box. There’s usually two of each.

8. Launch Pad: It is the first spawn point for players until the first emergency meeting. It is where the crew's ship is perched. It has two tasks:
- Fuel Engines: click the terminal on the left until the gas canister comes up. Hold down the bottom button until the engine is full.
- Run Diagnostics: use the console on the left and begin the task. It will take a long time, almost a minute, for this to go through. You can leave the terminal and come back, but the task will not register as complete until you select it one final time to choose the anomaly.

9. Medbay: It is the place where sick people reside. It consists of beds with a scanner and a vent between two beds. It has one Visual task:
- Submit Scan: stand on the central platform and begin the task. You must remain still during the scan for it to work.

10. Office: In Mira HQ map, the office is a medium-sized room located at the end of the North Hallway, across from Admin. It has a "The Henry Stickmin Collection" poster at the top right corner. It is in the upper area of the map. It has a vent in the upper left corner. The lights and one part of the comms sabotages can be fixed there. It has one task:
- Process Data: select the computer at the back of the room, then wait while the data uploads itself.

11. Reactor: In MIRA HQ map, the reactor is connected to the Laboratory, which contains a massive reactor with a claw that emits a laser (which can change color if the reactor is sabotaged), several computers, and two hand palms that are used to repair the sabotage of the reactor, just like on The Skeld. But, to get to the reactor, Crewmates must first go through Decontamination. A vent is located on the left below. It has three tasks:
- Start Reactor: go up to the actual reactor in the middle of the room. Once clicked, it’s a simple case of following the sequence of flashes until you’ve done this five times.
- Unlock Manifold: click the keypad on the left of the room, then tap the numbers 1-10 in sequence.
- Divert Power To: select the control panel, and then flip the switch to divert power to whichever room you need. From there, you just need to find the panel (the yellow arrow will guide you) and flip the connector in the center.

12.Storage: In this map, Storage is located near the Cafeteria. Inside the room is a bookcase which contains old materials, including the watering can, which is used to water the plants. It has one task:
- Water Plants: first, you need to select the watering can at the back of the room. This will bring up a new screen, where you’ll need to, um, click the watering can again. After this, head to the Greenhouse and click the plants on the right to finish the task.

13. Decontamination: It is where the crew members are decontaminated before going to the corridor that connects the Reactor and the Laboratory. It consists of an isolated room with yellow floors and black floors that flash blue, in addition to vaporizers and two doors that players can open by pressing the button on the side. When they enter, the door closes, and the place emits steam, and after that, the other door opens. It doesnt' have any tasks.

14. Hallway: The hallway is Y-shaped and has doorlog sensors at each of its exits. At its bottom right, there is a vent. The hallway is one of the busiest locations on the MIRA HQ map, so it is risky for An Impostor to kill there. However, by using the vent at the bottom right correctly, it is possible to escape after a kill. It has one task:
- Fix Wiring: click the panel then line up the wires across either side by matching the colors. After this, you’ll need to head to the Greenhouse and to the Reactor to finish the task.

The vent system: The vents on MIRA HQ are interconnected, meaning that an Impostor can use them to travel around the map more efficiently, unlike other maps. This makes venting a very efficient strategy for Impostors, as they can kill someone and appear pretty much anywhere else on the map.
